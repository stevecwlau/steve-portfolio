Project information

  • Title: Investigate the spatial association of suicide and socioeconomic variables in Hong Kong
    Download Full Study [PDF]
  • Data source: HK open data portal, the Copernicus Open Access Hub, NOAA National Centers for Environmental Information, WorldPop, Sqaurefoot Hong Kong, Midland Properties, Open Street Map (OSM)
  • Software used: QGIS, ArcMap, Tableau, Web Scraper (Chrome extension)
  • Skills: Correlation Analysis, LULC, heatmap, webscraping, geocoding, satellite imagery processing, weighted overlay analysis

As part of the requirements for my degree, I have successfully completed my final year research project based on GIS and suicide issues (awarded an A grade). The objective of the project was to investigate the spatial association of suicide and socioeconomic variables in Hong Kong.

This study applied geocoding, processing and raster extraction functionalities in Geographic Information System (GIS), using GIS software QGIS and ArcMap. Correlation analysis was also conducted in Tableau to identify the relationship between two socioeconomic variables and suicide incidence. The study also applied quartic heatmap interpolation technique to map suicide prevalence in the city. The result indicates that both urban density and socioeconomic deprivation are directly and significantly (at 0.001% level) related to suicide incidence. The suicide risk map based on finding of the correlation analysis was mapped using weighted overlay analysis.

The study concluded that the null hypothesis (H0) that there is no significant relationship between urban density/ socioeconomic deprivation and suicide incidence may be rejected at 0.01% (P<0.0001) level of significance. The top nine districts with the highest suicide prevalence are Sha Tin, Yuen Long, Tuen Mun, Kwai Tsing, Eastern, Sai Kung, Kwun Tong, Sham Shui Po and Tai Po.
